Super-Earths

Description:Super-Earths are planets with masses greater than Earth's but significantly less than those of ice giants like Uranus or Neptune.
Characteristics:

  • They can be rocky, like Earth, or have thick atmospheres like mini-Neptunes.
  • These planets may exist in a variety of environments, including habitable zones where liquid water could exist.
  • Super-Earths are more massive than Earth but do not reach the mass of gas giants.
    • Examples: Kepler-452b, often referred to as "Earth's cousin."
