Terrestrial Planets

Description: Terrestrial planets are rocky planets similar to Earth, Mercury, Venus, and Mars in composition.
Characteristics:

  • They have solid surfaces and are made up of metals and silicate rock.
  • Some terrestrial exoplanets may lie within the habitable zone of their star, where conditions could support liquid water.
  • These planets may have atmospheres, though not as thick as those of gas giants or mini-Neptunes.
    • Examples: Proxima Centauri b, an Earth-sized planet in the habitable zone of our nearest star.
